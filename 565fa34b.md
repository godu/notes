# Monoid

Let $(C, ⊗, I)$ a monoidal category. A triple $(M, μ, η)$ is called monoid where:

- $M$ is a object of category $C$
- $μ$ is a morphism $M ⊗ M \to M$ called *multiplication*
- $η$ is a morphism $I \to M$ called *unit*
